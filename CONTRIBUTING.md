# Contributing

ADD is a research framework in early development. All contributions are welcome — critiques and counterexamples are as valuable as additions.

## What we need

- **Counterexamples** — cases where the Model/Harness split breaks down or the decision rule gives the wrong answer. These sharpen the framework.
- **Examples** — concrete agentic systems (anonymized or synthetic) annotated with ADD concepts. See `examples/` for format.
- **Open questions** — additions to `OPEN-QUESTIONS.md` that identify gaps in the framework.
- **Concept refinements** — edits to `docs/` or `GLOSSARY.md` where a definition is imprecise, incomplete, or wrong.
- **Pattern documentation** — new topology patterns, decomposition patterns, or anti-patterns that are not yet covered.

## What we are not building here

- Implementation code or framework libraries (those belong in separate projects that ADD can inform)
- Model comparisons or benchmarks
- Tutorials or how-to guides for specific frameworks (LangGraph, AutoGen, etc.)

## How to contribute

1. Open an issue first for anything substantive. Describe what you've observed and why it affects the framework.
2. For small fixes (typos, clarifications), a PR is fine without an issue.
3. For new concepts or significant structural changes, discuss in an issue before writing.

## Principles for contributions

- Be precise. Vague concepts are worse than no concepts.
- Cite evidence. Empirical observations, production experience, and research references all count.
- Disagree openly. If an existing concept is wrong, say so directly with a reason.
- Keep it framework-level. ADD is about structure, not implementation.
